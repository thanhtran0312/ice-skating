My thesis project, where we (Dr. de Vries and Dr. Wurm at the University of Trento, CiMeC) aim to disentangle the representational dynamics of position and posture in the ventral and dorsal streams.

The main paper of dRSA method: de Vries, I.E.J., Wurm, M.F. Predictive neural representations of naturalistic dynamic input. Nat Commun 14, 3858 (2023). https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-023-39355-y

In that paper, they used dRSA (RSA + temporal components) to compare neural signals and theoretical models, such as pixelwise luminance, optical fllow, body posture (kinematic markers), etc., across time points. A peak at a negative lag (model precedes/predicts the neural signal in time) indicates a predictive signal; a peak at a positive lag (neural signal follows the model) indicates a reactive one.

Here, I use Ice Skating videos to add a position model to separate body posture (feature extraction from ViTPose) and body position (pelvis coordinate) to see what type of features is more representative in dorsal and ventral streams.

We may try semantic models for action classification (not sure yet).
